Session 3: ML Development and Algorithmic Bias

Fundamentals of AI for Business | Quality Matters-Aligned Course Outline

Session Overview
Duration: 2 hours (asynchronous, self-paced)
Module: 2 - AI Development and Execution
Prerequisites: Completion of Sessions 1-2 (AI fundamentals and ML techniques)
Case Study: COMPAS Recidivism Risk Assessment Algorithm

Session Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, learners will be able to:

  1. Describe the six-stage machine learning development cycle and explain the role of each stage (Knowledge/Comprehension - Bloom's Level 2)
  2. Classify nine types of ML biases into three reinforcing categories: data-to-algorithm, algorithm-to-users, and users-to-data (Analysis - Bloom's Level 4)
  3. Analyze how different types of bias emerge at specific stages of the ML development process (Analysis - Bloom's Level 4)
  4. Evaluate competing definitions of fairness (predictive parity vs. error rate balance) using statistical evidence (Evaluation - Bloom's Level 5)
  5. Apply bias identification frameworks to assess algorithmic systems in organizational contexts (Application - Bloom's Level 3)

Pre-Session Preparation 10 min

QM Standard 4.1 - Clear Instructions
  • Required Reading: Session 3 case overview (W42647 executive summary - 1 page)
  • Reflection Prompt: "Think about a recent decision made about you by an algorithm (loan approval, job screening, social media content). What information do you think it used?"

Learning Activities & Assessments

Activity 1: Case Introduction via Podcast 45 min

QM Standards 3.1, 4.3, 5.1 - Learning Objectives Alignment

Learning Objectives Addressed: 1, 4
Content: COMPAS bias podcast covering ProPublica vs. Northpointe debate
Format: Audio case study with guided listening prompts

Structured Listening Guide:

  • Minutes 0-10: COMPAS system overview - Note key stakeholders and system purpose
  • Minutes 10-25: ProPublica's argument - Identify their definition of fairness
  • Minutes 25-40: Northpointe's response - Compare competing fairness metrics
  • Minutes 40-45: Implications - Consider organizational decision-making

Checkpoint Assessment (QM 5.3 - Frequent Feedback)

Quick comprehension check: "In 2-3 sentences, explain the core disagreement between ProPublica and Northpointe."

Activity 2: Bias Framework Development 45 min

QM Standards 3.1, 4.2, 5.1

Learning Objectives Addressed: 2, 3, 5
Format: Interactive CustomGPT exercise with bias identification framework

Part A: ML Development Cycle Mapping (15 minutes)

  • CustomGPT guides students through building the 6-stage ML development cycle
  • Students identify where COMPAS bias emerged at each stage
  • Visual framework construction using provided template

Part B: Nine Types of Bias Classification (20 minutes)

  • Students categorize bias examples from COMPAS case
  • Interactive sorting activity: Data → Algorithm → User bias categories
  • Real-time feedback on categorization accuracy

Part C: Organizational Application (10 minutes)

  • Students apply framework to their own workplace/industry
  • Prompt: "Identify one potential bias risk in your organization's use of AI/data"
Formative Assessment (QM 5.2 - Meaningful Assessment)
Students submit their completed bias framework with one organizational example. Peer review using provided rubric.

Activity 3: Fairness Analysis & Synthesis 30 min

QM Standards 3.1, 5.4 - Synthesis and Application

Learning Objectives Addressed: 4, 5
Format: Video debrief with analytical reflection exercise

Part A: Statistical Fairness Comparison (15 minutes)

  • Instructor video explaining statistical concepts (FPR, FNR, PPV)
  • Side-by-side comparison of ProPublica vs. Northpointe metrics
  • Interactive calculator to explore trade-offs

Part B: Stakeholder Perspective Analysis (15 minutes)

  • Students take perspective of different stakeholders (judges, defendants, society)
  • Structured reflection: "Which definition of fairness would you prioritize and why?"
  • Consider organizational implications and ethical responsibilities
Summative Assessment (QM 5.1 - Aligned with Objectives)
Critical Analysis Essay (500 words): "Taking the role of a business leader implementing AI in your organization, which approach to fairness (ProPublica's or Northpointe's) would you adopt and how would you justify this decision to stakeholders?"
