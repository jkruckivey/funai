üìä Data Collection Bias (Stage 1)

What happens: Your training data doesn't represent the real population you'll serve.

COMPAS Example: Over-representation of Black defendants due to historical over-policing creates skewed training data.

Business Impact: Model learns from biased historical patterns and perpetuates them.

üßπ Data Preparation Bias (Stage 2)

What happens: Cleaning and processing choices introduce or amplify bias.

COMPAS Example: Using arrest records as proxy for criminal behavior, despite biased policing practices.

Business Impact: Proxy variables may not equally represent concepts across different groups.

ü§ñ Model Training Bias (Stage 3)

What happens: Algorithm design choices favor some groups over others.

COMPAS Example: Optimizing for overall accuracy led to better performance on majority group.

Business Impact: Technical optimization choices determine who benefits from your AI system.

‚úÖ Validation Bias (Stage 4)

What happens: Testing doesn't adequately check for fairness across groups.

COMPAS Example: Focus on overall accuracy rather than examining performance differences by race.

Business Impact: Bias problems go undetected until they become public crises.

‚öôÔ∏è Post-processing Bias (Stage 5)

What happens: Decision thresholds affect different groups unequally.

COMPAS Example: Risk score thresholds for "high/medium/low" categories impacted racial groups differently.

Business Impact: Same algorithm can produce different outcomes based on threshold choices.

üöÄ Deployment Bias (Stage 6)

What happens: Real-world usage differs from intended design.

COMPAS Example: Judges used scores inconsistently - some heavily relied on them, others ignored them.

Business Impact: Even unbiased algorithms create unfair outcomes if deployed inconsistently.

üìà Monitoring Bias (Stage 7)

What happens: Feedback loops reinforce and amplify existing bias.

COMPAS Example: Biased predictions lead to biased interventions, creating more biased data for future training.

Business Impact: Small initial biases compound into systematic unfairness over time.
